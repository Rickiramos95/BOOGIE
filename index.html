About

Born Artist Dubose in 1995, A Boogie Wit Da Hoodie emerged from Highbridge, The Bronx with a sound that blurs the lines between street, melodic, trap, and classic hip-hop.

The nickname was adopted in part from the 2002 movie Paid in Full. Rising in the post-Drake, post-Future era where bars vs. melody and street vs. pop collided, A Boogie carved out a lane that's impossible to ignore. Never one to settle, he constantly experiments—picking up a guitar with no lessons just to see what comes out, always chasing the next wave.

His key tracks—"Jungle," "Still Think About You," "Numbers," "Streets Don't Love You"—don't chart a course for hip-hop so much as leave a footprint. As he puts it: it's not even music at the end of the day—it's art.
